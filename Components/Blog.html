Dalol to Dashen

Ethiopia is located in the Northeast of Africa, bordering Somalia and Eritrea. It has Sudan, South Sudan, and Djibouti to its east, Kenya to its south and northwest and the Red Sea to its northeast. It's the 27th largest country in the world, with an area of 1.1 million square km. Ethiopia is largely mountainous country situated at an elevation of 2000m above sea level and lake region that lies in the Great Rift Valley zone. The Ethiopian highlands cover most of the country - these are some of the oldest mountains on Earth, over 12 million years old! The highest mountain peak is Ras Dashen at 4620m. A major river also flows through from north to south: the Blue Nile – one of two headwaters for the Nile River. Ethiopia is home to over 80 different ethnic groups with Amharic as the official language alongside English and other local dialects.

Dashen

The highest place in Ethiopia is Mount Ras Dejen, located on the Semien Mountains of northern Ethiopia. This volcano has an elevation of 4,533 metres (14,872ft) and sits atop a basaltic platform which has been created by thousands of years of volcanic activity. The mountain's peak is covered with snow for most of the year and provides stunning views as it rises out of a wide plain. Its unique shape gives it strong religious significance to local Ethiopians who consider this mountain sacred due to its massive scale and power over nature.
The lowest place in Ethiopia is the Danakil Depression, located in the Afar Region of Ethiopia. It is one of the hottest and driest places on earth, with an average temperature above 33.5 degrees Celsius and little to no rainfall. The 14,000-square mile lowland trough stretches across Ethiopia and also into neighboring Eritrea and Djibouti. It contains three salt lakes including Lake Asale which is 216 feet below sea level, making it the second lowest point in Africa after Lake Assal in Djibouti.

Recomended places to be visited

Ethiopia is an incredible destination with a diverse range of attractions from the historic rock-hewn churches of Lalibela to the Simien Mountains.This country has plenty of beautiful places for visitors to enjoy. One of which is Danakil Depression, it is situated in Northeast Ethiopia and surrounded by colourful salt plains and surreal volcanic landscapes. The church at Lalibela is also a breathtaking architectural wonder as ancient stone buildings have been carved directly into rock, some have an amazing façade culminating in soaring spires. Further Lake Tana offers spectacular beauty surrounded by forested hillsides and houses 37 ancient monasteries on its islands.
Lalibela is a historical town in Ethiopia which was the medieval capital of the Zagwe dynasty. It contains 11 rock-hewn churches from the 12th and 13th centuries, carved from rock and decorated with symbols representing eternity. These churches are a World Heritage Site, visited by thousands of visitors each year for their unique Ethiopian-style architecture, colorful frescoes, and rituals.
Axum is an ancient city located in Ethiopia. It is believed to be the center of the Queen of Sheba's kingdom and one of the oldest cities in Africa. It contains many ancient monolithic stelae, or obelisks, that are carved from a single solid piece of granite and weigh up to 170 tonnes. Axum is home to the Ark of Covenant, claimed by some to be the chest containing the original Ten Commandments. Axum is also known for its dramatic St Mary Cathedral which houses many religious artifacts such as a crown used by King Ezana. The city remains a major pilgrimage site for Ethiopian Christians.
Lake Tana is Ethiopia's largest lake, set at an elevation of 1,840m near the source of the Blue Nile River. It is home to an array of islands and monasteries. Nestled within its core is a beautiful collection of birds, fish, and other wildlife. A highlight for travelers is visiting the thundering Blue Nile Falls, consisting of 4 main sections (Kerssae Falls, infrastructure powerhouse Falls, Gilgel Gibe Falls 1, & Gall gel Gibe Falls 2). As part of visiting this spectacular site, observers also have access to a mesmerizing view over Lake Tana and beyond.
