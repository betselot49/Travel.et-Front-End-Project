What is new in Ethiopia

Ethiopia has completed the third filling of its mega-dam reservoir on the Blue Nile, Prime Minister Abiy Ahmed has announced, a development that could raise further tensions with downstream neighbours Egypt and Sudan. The announcement on Friday comes a day after Ethiopia said it had launched power production from the second turbine at the Grand Ethiopian Renaissance Dam (GERD).

The Addis Ababa “Beautifying Sheger” River Development Project is a scheme designed to improve the living conditions of people living near the river. The project started in the year 2020 and its goal is to restore the natural ecosystem of the river, improve sanitation infrastructure and create safe recreational spaces. The project aims at improving water quality and ecological balance, developing green spaces and parks, building bridges as well as other structures to increase public access and safety.

Ethiopia-Djibouti Transport Corridor: This major transport corridor is intended to improve the efficiency of railway links between Addis Ababa and Djibouti, accelerating trade in the region The transport corridor represents an important part of the development of the Greater Horn of Africa, connecting landlocked Ethiopia with the sea at Djibouti. The railway link is expected to greatly improve travel times and reduce transport costs by a factor of five. It also involves redeveloping jetties and constructing access roads that are planned to enhance trade opportunities between Ethiopia and Djibouti and further afield.
